üìñ Cybersecurity Glossary

Simple explanations of common terms

2FA / Two-Factor Authentication An extra layer of security that requires a second step (like a text code) to log in, even if someone has your password.
Antivirus Software that protects your computer by finding and removing malicious programs.
Backup A copy of your important files stored somewhere safe, so you can recover them if your device is lost or damaged.
Encryption Scrambling information so it can only be read by someone with the key to unscramble it.
HTTPS The 's' stands for secure. It means your connection to the website is encrypted. Look for the padlock icon.
Malware Malicious software designed to harm your device or steal information (viruses, spyware, ransomware).
Phishing Fake emails, texts, or websites that trick you into giving away personal information.
Ransomware A type of malware that locks your files and demands payment to unlock them.
VPN (Virtual Private Network) A service that creates a secure, encrypted connection over the internet, especially useful on public Wi-Fi.
Wi-Fi A wireless network that lets you connect to the internet without cables.
